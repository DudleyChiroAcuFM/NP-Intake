Sleep & Circadian
Sleep quality, duration, and disorders
Sleep is where the chain reverses on itself. Disrupted sleep is a downstream consequence of HPA dysregulation driven by the upstream gut and immune cascade — but it also becomes a powerful perpetuating driver in its own right. Poor sleep accelerates glucose dysregulation, amplifies inflammatory signaling, impairs hormone recovery, and directly degrades cognitive function. It creates a feedback loop that is hard to break without addressing both the upstream cause and the sleep disruption simultaneously. The brain also uses deep sleep to clear metabolic waste including amyloid — making sleep quality one of the most important variables in long-term neurological health.

Stress & Mental Health
Psychosocial history and mental health screening
Stress is both an upstream input and a downstream readout. Chronic psychological stress directly increases intestinal permeability, suppresses immune regulation, dysregulates cortisol, disrupts sleep architecture, and impairs mitochondrial energy production — making it a genuine upstream driver of the cascade, not just an aggravating factor. At the same time, the physiologic burden of a body in dysfunction generates anxiety, depression, and cognitive symptoms as downstream consequences. This bidirectional relationship means stress cannot be addressed in isolation — and it cannot be ignored while working on other systems.
